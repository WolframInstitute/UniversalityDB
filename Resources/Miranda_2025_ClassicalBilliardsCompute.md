# Classical Billiards Compute

Miranda-Ramos et al., 2025. arXiv:2512.19156.

Shows that planar billiard systems with polygonal obstacles can simulate any Turing machine. A billiard ball trajectory encodes the TM tape and head; obstacle geometry implements the transition function. Overhead: spatial sigma = O(1), temporal tau = O(|Q|) where |Q| is the number of TM states. Adds a new vertex type (PlanarBilliard) to the Universality Graph.
