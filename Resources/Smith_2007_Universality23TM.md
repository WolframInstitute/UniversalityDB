# Universality of Wolfram's 2,3 Turing Machine

Alex Smith, 2007. Wolfram Science Prize submission.

Proves the smallest known universal Turing machine (2 states, 3 colors) is computationally universal. The proof constructs a simulation chain: generic TM -> cyclic tag system -> Rule 110 -> (2,3) TM. The final step embeds Rule 110 glider dynamics into the (2,3) TM's tape evolution. Overhead bounds are large (exponential in input size) due to the chain of reductions.
